COD EXPLORATION WELL 7/11-7 SLOT A-6

IV. DISCUSSION

The well was completed in three formations: Zechstein dolomite, Triassic age Smith Bank Fm., and Jurassic age Ula Fm. Tests and stimulations were scheduled for each formation but were only successfully completed in the Jurassic zone. Restrictions on allowable working surface pressure (10,000 psi), temperature dependance and degradation of gels (325°F), deep deviation (15°), high down hole pressure (11,000 psi), tubing failure (poor heat treating), poor weather, and mechanical problems (proppant flow) contributed to the long duration of the testing.

A. BACKGROUND

A.1 GEOLOGY

The Cod 7/11-7 (A-6) exploration well was drilled from a fixed platform which gave some advantages (lower drilling costs, longer permissible time on site), and some disadvantages (restricted target, interruption of platform production, fixed size of casing, restrictions on surface pressure equipment). The platform is located in the midst of several other Jurassic - Lower Cretaceous age discoveries (Fig. 1). The hole was drilled to the southeast (Fig. 2) with an intended target in the Jurassic and Triassic age sandstones (Figs. 3 & 4). The well prognosis (Append. 1) indicates that this was to have been a straight hole and shows the prognosed depths to be considerable higher than was found by actual drilling (Fig. 5). The top of the Ula fm. was at -14,020 ft tvd (prognosed -13,000 ft tvd), the top Triassic was -14,145 ft tvd, and the top Zechstein was -15,086 ft tvd. These tops can be seen on the CPI (Append. 21) along with the RFT's, perforation depths and zones of interest. The composite log display chart (Fig. 6) shows the reasonable agreement between the CPI data and the GECO special core analysis (Append. 3) of water saturation Sw and porosity Ø for the Jurassic and Triassic pay sections. The gamma ray is higher in the Triassic age section and consequently indicates more clay or mica than in the Jurassic section though the PPCO Sedimentology, Petrography and Diagenesis report (Append. 4) indicates that just the opposite should be true. The PPCO report arrived long after the well was plugged and would have been of much more use had it arrived sooner.

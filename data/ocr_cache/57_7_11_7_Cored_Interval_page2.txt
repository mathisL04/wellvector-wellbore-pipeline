Sedimentology, Petrography and Diagenesis

of the Phillips 7/11-7 Cored Interval,

Norwegian North Sea

EPS Report No. 1753A

Summary

1. The Phillips 7/11-7 cored interval consists primarily of Jurassic and Triassic sandstone with minor interbedded shale. Hydrocarbon staining is present only in the Jurassic strata.

2. Bioturbation has thoroughly reworked the fine to medium grained Jurassic sands. The extent of organic activity suggests deposition in a nearshore marine shelf setting.

3. The Triassic cored interval consists of cyclic sandstone successions capped by siltstone. The sequence of primary sedimentary structures preserved is interpreted to be fluvial in origin. Braided channel conditions dominated deposition, although limited meandering channel point bar deposits are also present. The stacked cycles of fluvial deposition reflect persistent environmental conditions during Triassic time.

4. Fracturing/microfaulting occurs throughout the entire cored interval. The offsets observed are believed to be reflective of the force folding caused by the nearby salt movement.

5. The diagenetic histories of the Jurassic and Triassic are similar in the events recognized but differ in the extent of the alteration. Significant diagenetic events present include: (1) formation of silica overgrowths, (2) carbonate cementation, (3) dissolution of feldspar grains, and (4) precipitation of authigenic clay minerals.

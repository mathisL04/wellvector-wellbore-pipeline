# Introduction

Drilled from the Cod production platform, the Phillips 7/11-7 well cut 6 cores between the depths of 14,923' and 15,152'. Mr. G. Gershif, Stavanger office, and I described the slabbed core at GECO Laboratories in Stavanger. This report describes the sedimentology of the cored interval and documents the petrology of selected core chips. All depths used are those given by the driller and have not been shifted. The core is unoriented and was deviated approximately 14° from vertical.

Plate 1, located in the pocket at the end of this report, graphically displays the cored interval. For each lithofacies observed a brief lithologic description and inferred depositional environment are given. Petrographic sample locations and the analyses performed are also listed on the sedimentology log.

# Sedimentology

The 7/11-7 cores were taken in Jurassic and Triassic strata proximal to nearby salt movement. The rocks are clastic, dominantly sandstone with minor interbedded shale. Core 1 is a uniformly appearing Jurassic sandstone and will be discussed first.

The bioturbated sands of the Jurassic (14,923'-14,958') consist of fine to medium-grained beds in which the primary sedimentary structures have been disrupted by burrowing organisms. Figures 1 and 2 typify the appearance of the Jurassic sand. The sand is commonly medium to dark gray with occasional light-colored streaks. The subvertical burrow in Figure 1 has been preferentially cemented by dolomite. The extensive burrowing disrupting the clay laminations in Figure 2 is present throughout most of the Jurassic core.

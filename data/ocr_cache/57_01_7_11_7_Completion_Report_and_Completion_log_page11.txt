The well was opened on a 16/64" choke and the bottom hole pressure dropped from 11,487 to 6,461 psi and stopped flowing within 20 minutes. This pressure was 400 psi higher than that pressure for which the stimulation job was designed. After a brief 10.5 hr. buildup (P*= 11,440 psi), an injection test was made with the rig cementing pumps. The formation would only take 2 BPM at 9,000 psi, not nearly enough to permit calling the boat for stimulation. Following procedure (Append. 15) the upper two zones were perforated and another 40 ft perforating gun was lost. Again an injection test was made using the rig pumps. A rate of 3 BPM at 8,400 psi, the maximum output of the pump, was achieved and the stimulation vessel was called out.

An injection test was made using the stimulation vessel pumps on 23 September following delays caused by leaking chicsan lines. It acheived an injection rate of 11.9 BPM at 9,460 psi, beyond the minimum rate specified by Halliburton in their final bid. Unfortunately, unknown to the personnel in the platform control room, the supporting annulus pressure had dropped to zero (Fig. 11). The stimulation vessel was monitoring the injection rate closely and missed the display of the annulus pressure probe. Without the 2,000 psi backup annulus pressure, a substandard heat treated tubing joint failed and the tubing integrity was compromised. The joint parted and released the pressure (9,640 psi) into the annulus and associated lines which had only been rated to 3,000 psi. The annulus surface lines failed and were open to the atmosphere. The tubing parted and fishing operations started. The formation had demonstrated that it was capable of accepting the stimulation job and plans were made to recomplete the Triassic section.

While trying to retrieve the packer and tailpipe, problems were encountered. Unable to screw into the assembly, it was chased down the casing until it landed on the lost perforating guns. The top of the packer is at 15,640 ft md, 40 ft above the top of the lowest set of perforations. The packer could not be milled due to fear of damaging the casing, nor could it be retrieved. With one third of the available lower Triassic section isolated from the wellbore, the lower Triassic formation was abandoned due to mechanical problems without adequately being tested. The well was recompleted in the Jurassic and Triassic.

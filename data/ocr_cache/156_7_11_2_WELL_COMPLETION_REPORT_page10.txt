?Lower Eocene-?Paleocene Shale Unit: Thickness 70 meters (230 feet).

The interval is made up of light grey to greenish brown, soft to hard, slightly silty shales with reddish shales appearing in the lower portion. Traces of grey to brown, hard, dense, microcrystalline limestone occur throughout.

Upper Paleocene Sandstone Unit: Thickness 237 meters (778 feet).

The upper portion of the interval is composed of interbedded light grey to brown, very fine grained, micaceous, silty, slightly calcareous sandstones and siltstones with light grey to greenish grey shales. The base of the unit contains white to light brown, chalky to crystalline limestones.

Lower Paleocene Danian Limestone Unit: Thickness 126 meters (414 feet).

The upper portion of the interval consists of light tan to white to light grey, chalky, medium to hard limestone with thin stringers of white to grey very fine grained, calcareous sandstone and grey to greenish shale. The middle section consists of white to light grey, chalky to microcrystalline, brittle, dense limestone. The lower section consists of stringers of grey, very fine grained sandstone, grey to brown shale and white, chalky, dense limestone.

CONCLUSIONS

The Paleocene section was the only hydrocarbon objective in the 7/11-2X well. A net pay of 170 feet in the Upper Paleocene sandstone section (middle subdivision) was confirmed by DST No. 2, 9932 - 10,190', which gave a maximum flow rate of 43.18 MMCFGPD. The net pay was approximately 90 feet less than had been expected. The maximum measured liquid flowrate was 1390 BPD of condensate with a corresponding gas flowrate of 16.5 MMCFGPD. Condensate gravity ranged from $51.6^{\circ}-61^{\circ}$ API in the series of isochronal tests. Average gas-oil ratio was 11,900.

As in the 7/11-1X, no gas-water contact was established by the tests, nor is any evident by log analysis. At best, only high water saturations to +75% are calculable for individual sand stringers in the lower sand section immediately above the Danian. The possibility of an oil leg expressed in the Well Completion Report on 7/11-1X was not demonstrated nor indicated by tests or log analyses.
